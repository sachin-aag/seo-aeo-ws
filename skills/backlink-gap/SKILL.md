# Backlink Gap Finder

Backlinks are the third pillar of SEO. Ten links from relevant, real sites beats a thousand from junk directories. The most efficient way to get good backlinks isn't cold outreach to random blogs — it's finding the places that are already talking about your competitors and asking to be included.

That's what this skill does: find the gap and draft the ask.

## What you need from the user

- Their website URL or domain
- 2–3 competitor URLs or domains (the sites outranking them, not industry giants they can't compete with)
- Their niche or what they do in one sentence

## What to produce

### 1. Backlink gap analysis

You can't actually crawl the web, but you can help the user do it and interpret what they find.

**Give them the exact steps to run a gap analysis for free:**

Option A (Ahrefs free / paid):
```
1. Go to ahrefs.com/backlink-checker
2. Enter your domain → note the number of referring domains
3. Enter competitor 1 → note their referring domains
4. Use Ahrefs' "Link Intersect" tool:
   ahrefs.com/link-intersect
5. Enter your domain + 2 competitors
6. Click "Show link opportunities"
7. These are sites linking to both competitors but NOT to you
```

Option B (free, no account):
```
1. Google: link:[competitor-domain.com] -site:[competitor-domain.com]
   (This shows some sites linking to them — not comprehensive but free)
2. Look for: directories, industry publications, blog roundups,
   "best of" lists, local business sites, podcasts with show notes
3. Check each one: are you missing from pages that mention them?
```

### 2. Gap categories to look for

Guide the user on where gaps are most likely to appear and most worth closing:

**Directories:**
- Industry-specific directories (not generic link farms)
- Local business directories (Yelp, Google Business Profile, industry-specific)
- "Best of" pages for their city or niche

**Guest posts:**
Look at the by-lines on competitor content. If they're writing for publications in your niche and you're not, that's a direct gap.

**Roundup articles:**
Search for: `"best [category] tools"` or `"top [category] experts"` — find lists that include competitors but not you.

**Podcast show notes:**
If competitors are being interviewed in your niche, podcast episode pages link to guests. These are high-relevance, easy-to-earn backlinks.

**Resource pages:**
Search: `[your topic] + "resources"` or `[your topic] + "useful links"` — curated resource pages often link to the same handful of sites and are open to adding new recommendations.

### 3. Outreach email template

For each gap identified, draft an outreach email. Keep it specific, short, and human. Generic emails get ignored.

**Template:**

```
Subject: [Article/List name] — you might want to add [their business]

Hi [Name],

I was reading your [article title] on [publication] — great breakdown
of [topic].

I noticed you included [competitor name]. We do similar work at
[their business name] — [one specific differentiator in plain English].

Would you consider adding us? Here's the relevant page: [URL]

Happy to return the favour if there's a relevant place to link to
your work from ours.

[Name]
```

Customise this for each outreach. The subject line should reference the specific article, not be generic.

### 4. What NOT to do

Make sure the user understands which backlink tactics will get them penalised:

- **Private blog networks (PBNs):** Networks of fake sites created solely to build links. Google detects and penalises these.
- **Paid link placements on irrelevant sites:** If the link isn't editorially placed on a relevant page, it's spam.
- **Reciprocal link exchanges:** "I'll link to you if you link to me" at scale is a manipulative practice Google explicitly targets.
- **Fiverr / cheap backlink services:** Almost always PBNs or link spam. The short-term lift isn't worth the long-term penalty risk.

### 5. Local SEO note (if applicable)

If the user serves a local market, add:
- **Google Business Profile:** Set it up and fully complete it. It's the most important local citation.
- **NAP consistency:** Name, Address, Phone number must be identical across every directory (exact same format — "St." vs "Street" causes issues).
- **3 local citations per month:** Aim to add three relevant local directory listings every month until you have 20–30 solid ones.

```
### Backlink Gap Summary

**Your domain:** example.com
**Competitors analysed:** competitor1.com, competitor2.com

**Top gap opportunities:**
1. [Publication/directory name] — links to competitor1 and competitor2, not you
   Action: [what to do]

2. [Roundup article] — includes competitor but not you
   Action: [what to do]

3. [Podcast] — competitor was a guest
   Action: [what to do]

**Outreach drafts:**
[One email per opportunity]
```
